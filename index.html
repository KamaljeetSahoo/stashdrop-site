Install Stashdrop on your Mac

Download the app below and open it. A drive icon appears in your menu bar and quietly waits.

Pair your iPhone by scanning a code

Choose “Pair New iPhone” on the Mac; scan the QR with the iPhone app. Your phone pins the Mac’s certificate — no passwords, and no one else can send to your Mac.

Pick what to move, and go

Select photos and videos, tap Transfer. Watch the progress; keep using your phone while it runs. When it’s done and verified, free up the space if you want.

Questions

Do I need internet or an account?

No. Stashdrop works entirely on your local network (or your iPhone’s hotspot). There are no servers, sign-ups, or subscriptions.

What if I use iCloud Photos?

It still works great as a bulk transfer app — copy originals to your Mac. Just know that if iCloud Photos is on, deleting from your phone also removes from iCloud, so Stashdrop keeps the free-up step firmly optional and clearly labeled.

Is my Mac reachable when I’m out?

On the same Wi-Fi, yes. Away from Wi-Fi, turn on your iPhone’s Personal Hotspot and the Mac joins it automatically. A no-network peer-to-peer mode is on the roadmap.

What happens to a transfer that gets interrupted?

Nothing bad. A file only enters your vault after it fully arrives and its hash matches; partial transfers are discarded, and retrying never creates duplicates.

Where do my files end up on the Mac?

In a Stashdrop folder in your home directory, organized by date as ordinary files. Drag them anywhere — an external SSD, a backup, wherever you like.
